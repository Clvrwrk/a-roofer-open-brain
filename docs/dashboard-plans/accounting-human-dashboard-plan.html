What went wrong with the weekly snapshot prototype

The prototype copied the look of the AccuLynx email, but it did not yet satisfy the actual product requirement: the public route is returning 404, and the numbers are not durable links into information pages where a human can inspect the underlying records. The next version cannot ship as a static report. It needs route contracts, source contracts, and task-centered drill-through pages before any number appears.

Decisions now locked for the Accounting build

These answers replace the assumptions in v0.1 and should drive component scope, route design, role permissions, and source-system boundaries.

  • Lucinda is the primary Accounting reviewer. Chris does not need to see all packets. Chris sees escalation packets only.
  • Accounting v1 starts where margin is leaking. Price gaps, the invoice payment gate, and credit memo recovery come before AR, insurance, and job costing because vendor pricing erosion is the higher-value problem.
  • Snapshot clicks should support both full pages and panels. The URL should always represent the filtered detail page; the snapshot can also open a side panel for fast inspection.
  • QuickBooks remains Lucinda-controlled and isolated. The system can ask questions, surface recommendations, and record Lucinda-confirmed answers, but QuickBooks itself stays outside agent control and outside direct writes.
  • All internal business information may be shown before full WorkOS deployment. No additional pre-WorkOS PII redaction rule is required for these internal dashboard builds.
  • Dashboard and Slack are both human surfaces. The dashboard is the source of truth. Slack mirrors the same packet/action IDs for fast approval and follow-up.
  • Thresholds are confirmed. Use the standards defaults: credit memo expected value over $1,000 escalates to Chris, invoice over $50,000 requires AM review, AR 30/60/90 day escalations, credit memo follow-up at 7 and 21 days, and supplement denial over 25 percent escalates.
  • Price authority chain is Roberto -> Chris -> Lucinda. Roberto approves product/UOM/branch/line changes, Chris verifies final agreement authority, and Lucinda consumes approved pricing in invoice and credit memo packets.
  • Dashboards are department homes with workflow routes underneath. Accounting gets `/accounting`, with focused routes such as `/accounting/credit-memos/:id`.
  • This HTML is a throwaway alignment artifact. It should guide the build, but not become the maintained product spec.

Operating principles

Dashboard law for human-in-the-loop work

These rules apply to the weekly snapshot and to every Accounting dashboard below. They keep the UI from becoming a junk drawer of interesting but non-actionable data.

  • Department first, task second, metric third. The top-level navigation starts with Accounting, Operations, Sales, Marketing, Executive, and System. Metrics appear only after we know whose decision they support.
  • Every visible number is clickable. The destination must show the filtered records, source system, freshness, calculation, owner, current status, and next action.
  • Default views show exceptions and work, not trivia. Counts that do not require action move into a secondary evidence tab or periodic digest.
  • Humans approve external and irreversible actions. Agents may prepare packets, drafts, and recommendations. Human approval is required before vendor emails, customer messages, source table corrections, payment writes, trust-tier promotion, or any QuickBooks-related decision. QuickBooks remains under Lucinda's sole control.
  • Audit trail is part of the task, not a separate chore. Each dashboard includes who acted, what changed, why, before/after values, Auditor pass/fail, and follow-up due date where relevant.
  • Design follows the admin system. One content rail, tokenized spacing, flat contrast-layer surfaces, one flag-red primary action per viewport, smart-blue links, green status only, no decorative dashboard noise.

Accounting dashboard suite

Dashboards to build, in human-task order

The first suite should bias toward the problem already closest to live data: invoice safety, negotiated pricing, and credit memo recovery. These are first because pricing gaps and bad invoice gates erode margin. AR, insurance proceeds, job costing, and close remain standard Accounting work and follow after the margin-protection lane is working.

1. Accounting Command Center

Role-scoped work queue for Lucinda, Chris, Roberto, and read-only viewers. This is the Accounting home, not a metric poster.

Primary human: Lucinda Data: workflow tables plus invoice gates Route: /accounting
DecisionWhat needs my action now?
ActionOpen, approve, request changes
HiddenNon-action metrics by default

Default lanes: payment gate, credit memo packets, product/pricing blockers, AR escalations, month-close blockers, and escalation packets waiting on Chris.

2. Vendor Invoice Intake

Tracks portal exports, CSV/ZIP/PDF imports, missing files, extraction status, failed imports, source email/upload, and batch ownership.

Primary human: Lucinda Tables needed: vendor_invoice_batches Route: /accounting/vendor-intake
DecisionIs this batch complete?
ActionRetry, request file, start audit
HiddenLine math until import passes

Important because the current ABC handoff is ad hoc: portal export -> forwarded file -> manual Codex ingest. This dashboard replaces that with tracked Supabase state.

3. Invoice Payment Gate

Shows which vendor invoices are safe to pay, blocked, already paid through portal reconciliation, or marked with a recorded override.

Live base: invoice_documents, v_invoice_pricing_gate ABC mirror: abc_invoices, abc_invoice_lines Route: /accounting/invoices
DecisionCan this invoice be paid?
ActionApprove to pay, block, override
HiddenAll product catalog rows

This is the production-grade answer to "Current AR" or "Payments Received" style metrics: click the number, inspect the exact invoices, and see why the gate says yes or no.

4. Price Agreement Gap Queue

The existing `/abc-price-agreement-gaps` page becomes the reusable guardrail queue for missing SKU price lines, missing branch mappings, expired agreements, unverified agreements, UOM mismatches, and variance rows.

Live route exists Live ABC line data Route: /accounting/price-agreement-gaps
DecisionWhat pricing fix unblocks this line?
ActionAssign blocker to Roberto/Chris
HiddenInvoices with no pricing issue

This page already proves the shape: line-level record, why it was flagged, best agreement reference, variance, and human action. It should be refactored into the shared Accounting component library rather than left as a one-off.

5. Credit Memo Packet

One invoice per packet, exactly matching ACCT-035 through ACCT-046. Shows disputed lines, expected credit, draft email, Auditor math, and follow-up state.

Primary human: Lucinda Tables needed: credit_memo_requests and lines Route: /accounting/credit-memos/:id
DecisionApprove this packet?
ActionApprove, changes, reject, sent, received
HiddenUnrelated invoices or vendor history

The action bar has one primary action. External sending is never performed by the agent; the dashboard records that Lucinda sent it and starts the follow-up timer.

6. Product Match and UOM Stewardship

Blocks bad catalog trust by making product equivalency and UOM conversion a visible, auditable approval lane before any credit memo can go external.

Primary human: Roberto with Accounting Tables needed: product_equivalency_candidates Route: /accounting/product-matches
DecisionIs this product match instruction-grade?
ActionApprove, reject, needs evidence
HiddenWhole catalog browse

A packet should link to only the candidate SKUs that affect that packet. Broader catalog intelligence belongs in a separate search surface.

7. Vendor Region and Branch Mapping

Resolves PE regions, vendor account hierarchy, Ship-To accounts, branch access, and price-agreement branch matches. This is what prevents the wrong price zone from contaminating invoice math.

Live base: abc_regions, abc_vendor_branches Needs human mapping workflow Route: /accounting/vendor-regions
DecisionWhich agreement covers this branch?
ActionApprove mapping, flag exception
HiddenMap decoration unless needed

The visual territory map remains useful, but Accounting needs the narrow action view: ambiguous branches, invalid Ship-To accounts, missing agreement coverage, and the invoice lines blocked by each mapping.

8. AR Aging and Collections Readiness

Turns accounts receivable into a job-level action queue: balance age, last contact, signed contract, lien waiver posture, PM/AM owner, and next collection step.

Primary human: Lucinda plus PM/AM QB boundary: ask Lucinda Route: /accounting/ar-aging
DecisionWhat follow-up is safe now?
ActionRoute checklist, draft follow-up
HiddenPaid/healthy balances

The repo standard requires >30 day notification, >60 day collections readiness, and >90 day Chris escalation. Those thresholds become filters, not footnotes. Any QuickBooks fact is captured as a Lucinda-confirmed answer or recommendation, not a direct agent read/write.

9. Insurance Proceeds and Depreciation Recovery

Tracks ACV received, RCV holdback, deductible, supplement status, certificate of completion, denial reasons, and 90-day recovery risk.

Base table: insurance_claim AccuLynx hydration needed Route: /accounting/insurance-proceeds
DecisionWhat claim money is still recoverable?
ActionRoute supplement or RCV task
HiddenNon-insurance jobs

This is where the AccuLynx snapshot's claim and payment numbers should eventually drill through for insurance jobs.

10. Change Order Financial Review

Catches performed work that lacks signed authorization before it appears in a final invoice or financial close.

Primary humans: PM/AM, Lucinda Needs signed change-order workflow table Route: /accounting/change-orders
DecisionCan this amount enter revenue?
ActionAttach signature, reject, hold
HiddenOps logs unrelated to cost

Pulls from Ops observations and CompanyCam evidence only when they explain the financial change. It should not become a daily-log viewer.

11. Job Costing and Margin Risk

Compares contract amount, material invoices, ABC orders/shipments, labor/sub costs, change orders, and gross margin against the floor.

Base: job, abc_orders, abc_order_lines QB boundary: Lucinda-controlled Route: /accounting/job-costing
DecisionIs this job financially at risk?
ActionInvestigate variance, route close blocker
HiddenHealthy closed jobs by default

The first version should show material-cost confidence clearly so a human knows what is real, estimated, or missing.

12. Financial Close and Month-End Close

The close dashboard brings together job close, warranty, QB references, pending supplements, AR, AP, WIP, missing receipts, and unresolved vendor exceptions.

Primary human: Lucinda Chris: escalation only Needs close packet workflow Route: /accounting/close
DecisionCan we close this period/job?
ActionClear blocker, approve packet
HiddenEverything already reconciled

This dashboard should never be a spreadsheet clone. It is a blocker list with evidence links and a final close packet. Any QuickBooks reference is a Lucinda-confirmed fact or recommendation, not a direct agent-controlled integration.

13. Accounting Audit Log and Vendor QC

Shows all human decisions, agent proposals, overrides, math checks, rejected packets, vendor no-response patterns, and standard-change candidates.

Primary humans: Chris, QC owner Tables needed: agent_action_log, admin_review_packets Route: /accounting/audit-log
DecisionIs this a process problem?
ActionOpen DMAIC, update standard
HiddenRoutine pass events unless sampled

This is the dashboard that prevents silent drift. It should summarize patterns and link back to exact packets, not replay every row as noise.

Recommended build order

How to get from plan to working dashboards

This order keeps the product honest: route and data contracts first, components second, dashboards third, Slack mirroring later.

Fix deployment and route registry

Serve `app/command-center` at `cc.proexteriorsus.net`, verify `/healthz`, add a route registry for Accounting and weekly snapshot drilldowns, and fail closed when source data is missing.

Define Accounting data contracts

For every metric and dashboard, define source tables, filters, freshness, row-level destination, permitted roles, and write contract. No route should ship from mock data.

Build the Accounting component library

Implement and test the reusable components listed above with Supabase-backed fixture routes. Each component should have empty, loading, degraded, live, and permission-denied states.

Refactor the ABC price-agreement gap page

Use the existing live page as the first component-library consumer. Preserve the good data logic, replace bespoke UI with shared primitives.

Build payment gate and credit memo packet workflow

Add workflow tables/endpoints, then build invoice gate, packet detail, Lucinda approvals, human sent confirmation, follow-up, and receipt closure.

Build the remaining Accounting dashboards

Vendor intake, product matches, region mapping, AR aging, insurance proceeds, change orders, job costing, close, audit log, and QC.

Wire Slack as a mirror, not the source

Dashboard and Slack both support human action, but the dashboard/Supabase workflow state is authoritative. Slack posts the same packet/action IDs for fast approval and follow-up after the browser workflow is trustworthy.

Decisions captured from Chris

These are the direct answers behind the locked decision list above. Keep this section as traceability for the throwaway review artifact.

1. Who is the primary Accounting reviewer?

Lucinda is primary. Chris does not need to see all packets, only escalation packets.

2. Is vendor cash recovery the first Accounting lane?

Yes. AR, insurance, and job costing remain standard work processes, but price gaps, the invoice gate, and credit memo recovery are higher value because they erode margin.

3. What should a snapshot click open?

Use both: a full detail page with the same filters represented in the URL, plus an optional side panel from the snapshot for quick inspection.

4. What is the real QuickBooks source today?

Lucinda answers all QuickBooks questions. QuickBooks remains isolated from the agent process and solely controlled by Lucinda. The dashboard may ask questions and surface recommendations, but not directly control QB.

5. How much PII can the internal dashboard show before WorkOS is live?

All information can be shown before full WorkOS is deployed. No extra internal display restriction is required for this build phase.

6. Should external-send confirmation live in dashboard only or Slack too?

Dashboard and Slack are both allowed human surfaces. The dashboard is the source of truth; Slack is a mirror of the same packet/action IDs.

7. Confirm thresholds.

Confirmed. Defaults found in standards: credit memo expected value over $1,000 escalates to Chris, invoice over $50,000 requires AM review, AR 30/60/90 day escalations, credit memo follow-up at 7 and 21 days, supplement denial over 25 percent escalates.

8. Who approves price agreement line changes?

Roberto approves, Chris verifies, and Lucinda consumes. Roberto owns the product/UOM/branch/line decision; Chris verifies the final agreement authority.

9. Should dashboards be department homes or workflow apps?

Use department homes with workflow routes underneath. Example: `/accounting` -> `/accounting/credit-memos/:id`.

10. Should this HTML become a maintained product spec?

Treat this HTML as a throwaway alignment artifact, not a maintained product spec.
