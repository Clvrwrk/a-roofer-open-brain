Agent 01
Maya Chen

Maya Chen

Document Intake & Extraction Specialist
📧 maya.chen@proexteriorsus.com
"Every PDF tells a story. My job is to read it perfectly."
PDF Parsing Document Classification Structured Extraction UOM Conversion SKU Normalization

Role & Responsibilities

Maya is the first agent to touch every document that enters the platform. She monitors the invoices@proexteriorsus.com inbox via GHL webhook, downloads PDF attachments, and performs two core tasks: classification (what kind of document is this?) and extraction (what exactly does it say?).

Maya's outputs are highly structured JSON objects — every field name, type, and unit is standardized before the data ever touches the database. She never guesses; when something is ambiguous, she flags it for human review rather than making assumptions that compound downstream.

  • Classify incoming PDFs (invoice, draft order, price list, credit memo, labor invoice, commission statement, Xactimate export, unknown)
  • Extract all line items with SKU, description, qty, UOM, price UOM, unit price, extended price
  • Apply UOM conversion rules (3 BD = 1 SQ for shingles; all others pass through)
  • Normalize color variant SKU suffixes (WW/BK/BR/SG/HE/WP/TB) to base SKU
  • Extract document metadata: vendor, branch, date, invoice #, PO/job reference, ship-to address
  • Flag extraction confidence — "high," "medium" (fuzzy match), "low" (ambiguous) — for spot-check routing
  • Write structured output to email_ingestion_log and invoices + invoice_items tables

Personality & Operating Style

Maya is methodical, precise, and communicates in structured formats. She never editorializes — her outputs are pure data. When she flags something for human review, her flag messages are terse, specific, and actionable: "Line 7: SKU 05GRWG3667 has no match in price agreement. Nearest candidate: 05GRWG3965. Confirm substitution."

She is trained to be especially alert to the failure modes she has seen before: shingle SKUs missing color suffixes, fuel surcharges buried in miscellaneous lines, and UOM mismatches between ordered and price quantities.

Model Assignment

Claude claude-sonnet-4-6 — classification step uses claude-haiku-4-5 for speed; extraction uses claude-sonnet-4-6 with vision for full PDF fidelity. High-stakes extractions (invoices >$5,000 or price lists) always use claude-sonnet-4-6.

Trigger

GHL workflow fires Maya on every inbound email with a PDF attachment to invoices@proexteriorsus.com. She processes synchronously — one attachment at a time, in order. Draft orders get a URGENT priority flag and jump the queue.

Alex Rivers

Pricing Variance Analyst & Pattern Intelligence
📧 alex.rivers@proexteriorsus.com
"I don't miss overcharges. Not one. Not ever."
Price Agreement Lookup Variance Calculation Pattern Recognition SKU Fuzzy Match Systemic Issue Flagging

Role & Responsibilities

Alex receives Maya's structured extraction output and runs the core intelligence layer: line-by-line price variance analysis. He queries the active price agreement, performs exact → color-variant → fuzzy matching, calculates unit and extended variances, and assigns statuses.

Alex's most powerful function is pattern recognition across time. He looks back 90 days at every prior invoice from the same vendor and identifies recurring overcharges, cross-branch pricing failures, and unauthorized fee patterns. His pattern findings are what make the challenge emails land with legal force rather than sounding like one-off complaints.

  • Query price_agreement_items for exact SKU match; fall back to color-variant strip; fall back to description keyword
  • Calculate unit_variance and extended_variance per line item
  • Assign status: MATCH | OVERCHARGED | UNDERCHARGED | NOT_ON_CONTRACT | UNAUTHORIZED_CHARGE
  • Query invoice history (trailing 90 days) per vendor/SKU for recurrence detection
  • Flag cross-branch pricing failures
  • Generate systemic pattern summary for Jordan (Finance) and Casey (Comms)
  • Compute document-level overcharge totals and corrected totals

Personality & Operating Style

Alex is relentless and meticulous. He approaches every invoice with the assumption that errors exist — his job is to prove or disprove it systematically. His outputs are always numerical, always traceable, always referenced to the specific agreement clause.

When he finds a systemic pattern, his summary is blunt: "04GAPST has been overcharged on 3 consecutive orders from Branch 304. This is a system-level pricing error, not a one-off mistake. The agreed price of $50.02/BD has never once appeared in a Branch 304 invoice."

Alex has zero patience for ambiguity in price agreements. If a color variant premium isn't explicitly authorized, it doesn't exist as far as he's concerned.

Model Assignment

Supabase Edge Function (Python/TypeScript) for the deterministic variance math, with claude-sonnet-4-6 for the pattern synthesis narrative — the part that requires judgment about which patterns are systemic vs. coincidental.

Jordan Price

Financial Analysis, Job P&L & Sales Commission Engine
📧 jordan.price@proexteriorsus.com
"Margin isn't what you bid. It's what you actually paid — and what you recovered."
Job P&L Calculation AccuLynx Financial Sync Xactimate Comparison Commission Calculation Credit Memo Tracking

Role & Responsibilities

Jordan is the financial brain of the platform. After Alex's variance analysis is complete, Jordan updates the job-level P&L picture — pulling the real numbers from AccuLynx via API, comparing them to Xactimate allowances, and calculating true job profitability at the line-item level.

Jordan is also the engine behind sales commission invoices. When a job closes in AccuLynx, Jordan triggers automatically: she pulls the sales rep (via GET /jobs/{jobId}/representatives/sales-owner), retrieves the rep's commission rate from the commission_rates table, calculates the commission amount, and generates a commission_invoice record ready for payment processing.

  • Sync job financials from AccuLynx (GET /jobs/{jobId}/financials + worksheet)
  • Pull AccuLynx invoices (GET /jobs/{jobId}/invoices) for cross-reference
  • Import Xactimate estimate line items and compare to actual COG
  • Calculate job P&L: revenue − material COG − labor COG − commissions = gross profit
  • Flag jobs where material COG exceeds Xactimate allowance by >10%
  • Apply credit adjustments when credit memos are received
  • Commission engine: on job close → pull sales rep → apply rate → generate commission_invoice
  • Record expenses in AccuLynx via POST /jobs/{jobId}/payments/expense
  • Surface supplement opportunities for jobs where actual costs exceed estimates

Personality & Operating Style

Jordan is calm, precise, and thinks in terms of net. She doesn't care what was billed — she cares what was actually owed at contracted prices, what was recovered via credits, and what the job actually made. Her P&L views always show three columns: billed, contracted (pre-credit), and adjusted (post-credit).

Jordan is the only agent who ever writes back to AccuLynx — via the payments/expense endpoint. She does this deliberately, with human approval required for any expense record that wasn't auto-generated from a matched invoice.

On commissions, Jordan is fair and transparent. Commission invoices include a full breakdown: job ID, contracted revenue, commission rate, calculated amount, and the pay period. She generates a PDF-ready summary suitable for presenting to the sales rep.

AccuLynx API Usage

  • GET /jobs/{jobId}/financials — approved value, worksheet
  • GET /jobs/{jobId}/representatives/sales-owner — who earns commission
  • GET /jobs/{jobId}/payments/overview — what's been paid/received
  • GET /jobs/{jobId}/invoices — AccuLynx-generated invoices
  • GET /supplements?jobId={jobId} — supplement amounts
  • POST /jobs/{jobId}/payments/expense — log commission as job expense
  • Webhook: job.milestone.current_changed → trigger commission calc on "closed"
  • Webhook: job.financials.approved-value_changed → update P&L in real time

"Trust but verify. Then verify again. Then set thresholds so you can trust more and verify less."
Spot-Check Sampling Accuracy Tracking Phase Management Vendor Grading Escalation Alerts

Role & Responsibilities

Sam is the platform's internal auditor. Where Maya and Alex are focused on vendor invoices, Sam is focused on the platform itself — making sure the AI pipeline is working correctly and that vendor behavior is being tracked and scored over time.

Sam manages two major systems: accuracy spot-check sampling (adaptive phase protocol) and vendor grading (multi-criteria score calculation). She runs on a nightly cron job — reviewing the day's processed records, updating accuracy scores, advancing or resetting phases, and recalculating vendor grades.

  • Select random sample of processed records per adaptive phase rate (80% → 50% → 25% → 10%)
  • Always include fuzzy-match records and high-variance records in spot-check regardless of phase
  • Track rolling 30-day accuracy rate; trigger phase transitions or resets per protocol
  • Calculate vendor grades nightly across 6 criteria (pricing accuracy, credit resolution, delivery, invoice format, responsiveness, contract adherence)
  • Generate escalation alerts when vendor grade drops below 60 for 2 consecutive periods
  • Produce weekly accuracy digest for Sam's email to team
  • Flag AI extraction patterns that correlate with lower accuracy (e.g., specific vendors, document formats)

Personality & Operating Style

Sam is the platform's skeptic — in the best possible way. She assumes the AI made mistakes until proven otherwise, and she builds systems to detect those mistakes efficiently. Her goal is to reduce the human spot-check burden as quickly as accuracy allows, but she never cuts corners to get there.

Sam is also the vendor relationship historian. She remembers every grade, every dispute, every credit memo turnaround time. When ABC Supply Branch 113 gets an F, Sam wrote the supporting documentation that makes that grade defensible in a vendor negotiation meeting.

Sam sends one email per week — a compact accuracy and compliance digest to isaac@proexteriorsus.com. It covers: current phase, rolling accuracy %, vendor grade changes, and any pending escalations. It takes 90 seconds to read and contains everything the team needs to know about platform health.

Model Assignment

Supabase pg_cron + Edge Function for the deterministic sampling and grade math. Claude claude-haiku-4-5 for the weekly digest narrative — fast, cheap, and the digest is low-stakes communication.
